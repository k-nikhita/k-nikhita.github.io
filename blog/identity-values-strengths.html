Identities, Values & Strengths
A summary of who I am and what I bring to my learning journey. November 17, 2021


"Know thyself."
- Socrates


Personal Strengths. Why bother?


If you've attended school and have some work experience under your belt that involved job interviews, I am sure you've heard of personal strengths (& weaknesses). But I find that with all the messages we're bombarded with from social media and a capitalist society that constantly pushes the ideals of "hustle culture", it can be quite confusing and daunting to look inward and see ourselves for who we really are instead of who we are told we should be. This is why, I think it is really important to reflect on one's experiences often enough to get a sense of the values and the individual strengths that we consistently demonstrate in various life situations.

According to Positive Psychology, personal strengths are defined as our built-in capacities for particular ways of thinking, feeling and behaving.



My Character Strengths


A few months ago, I joined an online course on Coursera, called "The Science of Well-Being". In the first week of that course, I was required to identify my Character Strengths by taking an online test available on the VIA website. The test comprised of 120 questions and took about 30 minutes to complete, at the end of which I got a report that gave me a ranking of my 24 strengths. We were then asked to take note of our top 5 strengths and use one selected strength in a new way, for each day for the following week.

My top 5 "character strengths" according to the VIA assessment are:

  1. Curiosity
  2. Creativity
  3. Honesty
  4. Love
  5. Humour

Now, over the years, I have taken several personality tests with unsurprising results because I found that most of the assessments seemed to be consistent with how I viewed myself at the time. However, I did find it interesting that my personal strengths continued to evolve over time as I worked different jobs, moved to a different country, made friends from different cultures and fell in love with a German guy! What I found really cool about the exercise from Coursera was that I was encouraged to look at my strengths and apply them differently each day, thereby making me realise how important it is to for our personal happiness that we actively look for opportunities where we can apply our strengths instead of getting intimidated my challenges that sometimes test our strengths and bring our weaknesses to the fore.



How My Character Strengths Have Defined Me


Curiosity:

It has taken me a while to fully embrace that curiosity is indeed my biggest strength. In the past, I used think that for one to be a curious person, they had to be curious about almost everything and that somehow it had to be a trait of only the very smart people, thereby disqualifying me. However, in the last 13 months of quitting my full time job, I have discovered that I thrive in situations where my curiosity is piqued. I am generally very extroverted in nature, but I particularly seek and crave the company of people that know things that I don't, people that have significantly different life experiences for my own and people that can change my views on things. I love getting into new hobbies and activities, and generally keep an open mind to things I can do with my life. And looking back at the last 15 years of my working career, it is this hunger for new experiences that made jump at opportunities that my younger self could not have possibly foreseen.

A year ago, I would have laughed at the idea of learning to code! But over the last year, the more I read about the future of work and the lack of diversity in the world of tech, the more intrigued I was. It only took ONE serendipitous conversation with a friend (who has also studied at Dev Academy), for me to rush home and apply for the Web Development Bootcamp! I am in Week 2 of the program and love it so much that my brain feels stretched in all the right ways at the end of the day. I know that curiosity is going to be my biggest fuel on my learning journey at EDA. This also means that I might potentially get distracted by something else that is interesting, but to counter that, I have been focusing on developing good learning habits such as monotasking, timeboxing and staying consistent right from the start.


Creativity:

I used to think that for one to be creative, they had to be good at the arts or had to be special or talented in some way. But lately, I have come to realise that creativity is more about how you perceive the world around you and if you are able to make connections between seemingly unrelated phenomena, and come up with solutions. When I look back at all the different jobs I have done, hobbies I have pursued (and given up on), the people I have made friends with and the ways with which I have overcome challenges in my life, I see creativity as a defining trait of my personality.

I am enjoying the challenge of building a learning blog SO MUCH right now! I look at websites differently now, and think about what they might have done to incorporate a specific design/interactive element. Each morning when I wake up, my mind is full of ideas and improvements I would like to make to this blog and I look forward to putting those in action. The downside to that is that I sometimes tend to get into deep rabbit holes with design or feature related things but timeboxing really helps me get out of that trap!


Honesty:

I have three values that I hold dearly and use as a guide for my interactions with people and life in general: being authentic, open and effective. I can be remarkably good at what people call "oversharing" but I truly believe in the power of telling honest, authentic stories. I strive to be open with people as much as possible because that is what I prize in others.
I am also very honest with myself and this helps my learning pursuits a lot. While perhaps bordering on being over-critical of myself, I am quite aware of my lesser strengths when it comes to learning and unlearning. I find that sharing openly about them helps me get over them rather quickly, and as with many other things in life, that it's just a matter of practice!


Love & Humour:

I am your stereotypical "happy-go-lucky" kind of girl. I value close connections and love to have a good laugh over things. It's a lucky thing to be able to see the light side of things, especially in shitty situations.



How My Culture Has Influenced My Identity


I grew up in a big family in India, where I lived with my parents, younger brother, grandparents, uncle, aunt and cousins for a good part of my life. This taught me the values of kindness, generosity and adaptability at a very young age. Even though we lived in a pretty traditional set-up with rigid gender roles, my mother also worked full-time while tending to all the responsibilities that came with being a woman in an Indian household. Growing up, she was a huge inspiration to me and a symbol of hardcore tenacity with her ability to just get on with things, and this has shaped how I see my own strength and potential. For many years, I strove to be as strong as her, and worked hard to prove myself in my career and personal life.

My father is a practical man. From growing up in a remote, rural village in Northern India to moving to a big city like Mumbai for his work and eventually retiring early to return to the same village recently, he has been on quite a transformative journey himself. The younger me, while petrified of my father's occasional outbursts of anger, was quite impressed by his ability to keep calm in tense situations that required rational thinking. This is something I took on board quite early in my life, and have striven to emulate that ever since.
