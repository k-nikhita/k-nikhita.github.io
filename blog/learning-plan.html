Learning Plan
This learning plan describes how I aim to operate while studying at Dev Academy. November 17, 2021


"If you fail to plan, you are planning to fail."
- Benjamin Franklin


Long Term Goal

My long-term goal is to learn several coding languages well enough, so that I can look at real-life problems around me and actually solve them them through the creative use of code. For a job/work, I would ideally like to be involved with a company that is making a meaningful impact in any of the following areas: climate change, public health, arts & culture.


Strengths and Limitations in relation to Learning

I am incredibly passionate about learning and as mentioned in my previous blog, curiosity is going to be my fuel on this learning journey. Some of the other strengths I have recently developed include the ability to focus on a single task and work without distractions.

My curiosity for learning sometimes takes me into deep rabbit holes. And the downside of my passion is that I often get into things with a lot of passion, pour myself into them and eventually run out of steam.


Skills I would like to develop while at Dev Academy

I want to develop and strengthen my learning habits during the course of this program. In addition to that, some of the skills I'd like to work on include leadership, problem-solving and big-picture thinking.


Biggest Technical Challenge?

No idea.


Biggest Non-Technical Challenge?

Dealing with imposter syndrome and perfectionism.



Strategies for Managing Course Workload


Working productively:

During bootcamp:

  • I will aim to stay present and focused in all sessions. I will do this by ensuring that I show up to class well-rested, fed and hydrated.
  • I am a morning person, so I will try to use them for any learning I need to catch up with, by waking up early.
  • When studying on my own, I will continue to use the practices I did during Foundations, such as aiming to study in 45 minute blocks, without any distractions, using Toggl to track study time, taking breaks to refresh my mind, avoiding procrastination, etc.
  • I will delegate more household chores to my husband to ensure that I can maximise study and focus time during the week and use weekends to refresh and reset.
  • I will use the check-ins and tech-ins at bootcamp to communicate any struggles/concerns to my facilitators to ensure that I get the support I need instead of wasting time feeling stuck.

    • A few reminders for myself:

  • Have fun!
  • Be present.
  • Stay committed to being on top of coursework.
  • Make friends!
  • Have faith in the learning process.
  • Avoid perfectionism and procrastination. "Done is better than perfect!"
  • Use weekends to REST & RECHARGE.


    • Dealing with Stress:

    I find that planning and communication are key to dealing with stress. 18 weeks pregnant at the time of updating this plan, I will have a few other baby-related things going on with my body and personal life, that are a bit hard to anticipate at this stage, but I am aiming to communicate closely with the facilitation team at EDA so that there are no surprises.

    My plan involves starting each day by identifying what the priority for the day is. I am fortunate to have a great support system in my husband and there are very few tasks that I cannot delegate to him. Some of these include checking in with my own needs, looking after myself and studying for this course.

    If I find myself in a situation where I am struggling to cope, I will:

    • communicate with my husband, other learners and facilitators (the situation will dictate the order)
    • focus and act on what I can control
    • identify the bare minimum that needs to be done for me to move to the next step
    • amend and revise my learning plan to get me where I need to be!


    Seeking Help in a Timely Way:

    I will keep an eye on how I am tracking with the curriculum and communicate often with the facilitation team.


    Expectations from the Facilitation Team:

    To support me in my learning in a non-judgmental way but also not be afraid to give me open and honest feedback that will help me grow. I value and thrive on feedback and get easily demotivated by empty words of praise. I am here to learn and improve.


    Scheduling Information:

    I am currently not working and have no kids (yet). This makes it a very ideal time for me to study. I am due with my first baby in June, leaving me plenty of time to study from January to March.

    Outside of studying, I try to keep fit with pole dancing lessons and would really like to be able to keep up with those while still at bootcamp. However, I will try and use the weekends for that to ensure that I am not cramming too much into the week!

    I will do my best to do most of my studying in the week, leaving the weekends to relax, refresh & socialise with friends. However, when things pile up, I won't have any qualms putting social & other commitments on hold briefly so I can catch up.
