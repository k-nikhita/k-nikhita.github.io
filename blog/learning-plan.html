Learning Plan
This learning plan describes how I aim to operate while studying at Dev Academy. November 17, 2021


"If you fail to plan, you are planning to fail."
- Benjamin Franklin


Long Term Goal

My long-term goal is to learn several coding languages well enough, so that I can look at real-life problems around me and actually solve them them through the creative use of code. For a job/work, I would ideally like to be involved with a company that is making a meaningful impact in any of the following areas: climate change, public health, arts & culture.


Strengths and Limitations in relation to Learning

I am incredibly passionate about learning and as mentioned in my previous blog, curiosity is going to be my fuel on this learning journey. Some of the other strengths I have recently developed include the ability to focus on a single task and work without distractions.

My curiosity for learning sometimes takes me into deep rabbit holes. And the downside of my passion is that I often get into things with a lot of passion, pour myself into them and eventually run out of steam.


Skills I would like to develop while at Dev Academy

I want to develop and strengthen my learning habits during the course of this program. In addition to that, some of the skills I'd like to work on include leadership, problem-solving and big-picture thinking.



Strategies for Managing Course Workload


Working productively:

I am aiming to study from Monday to Friday, striving to finish most of the current sprint work by Thurday, so that I can use Friday to reflect on my learning, attack the stretch material or move on to the material for the next sprint.

I am a morning person, so I try my best to do as much of my learning then.

As much as possible, I try to study in 45 minute blocks. I do this by starting a timer on my phone for each session and using Toggl on my desktop to track overall time. I have a tendency to treat work like a marathon, which I have learnt over time is not very effective. Studying in 45 minute blocks makes the overall project I am working on less daunting, so I am less likely to procrastinate and also allows me to take frequent breaks to refresh my mind.

When I study, I do my best to keep distractions to a minimum. Some of the things I do include putting my phone on silent mode and face down; working on a virtual desktop dedicated only to coding work; not checking Slack/social media when in a focus session; letting the husband know that I am in "focus mode" and not to be disturbed unless it's something urgent.

I start each day and sprint with a rough idea of how many 45 minute blocks it would take me to finish the work cut out for me. And then, I start chipping away at it. Starting this way makes me more mindful of what I want to achieve from a study session, day and sprint overall, and helps me focus better.

I am a strong communicator and good at asking for help when I am stuck. I take deadlines seriously and communicate in advance when I foresee not being able to meet them.

Dealing with Stress:

I find that planning and communication are key to dealing with stress. I am currently 11 weeks pregnant (Yay! First time mum too!), so I am anticipating that things will change a LOT for me in the coming weeks as I deal with all the crazy changes happening to my body and ride the hormonal rollercoaster of emotions, while managing to keep with the course curriculum.

My plan involves starting each day by identifying what the priority for the day is. I am fortunate to have a great support system in my husband and there are very few tasks that I cannot delegate to him. Some of these include checking in with my own needs, looking after myself and studying for this course.

If I find myself in a situation where I am struggling to cope, I will:

  • communicate with my husband, other learners and facilitators (the situation will dictate the order)
  • focus and act on what I can control
  • identify the bare minimum that needs to be done for me to move to the next step
  • amend and revise my learning plan to get me where I need to be!


Seeking Help in a Timely Way:

I will keep an eye on how I am tracking with the curriculum and communicate often and early with my facilitator, Joseph Quested. By this, I also mean that I will NOT leave things to the last minute!


Expectations from the Facilitation Team:

To support me in my learning in a non-judgmental way but also not be afraid to give me open and honest feedback that will help me grow. I value and thrive on feedback and get easily demotivated by empty words of praise. I am here to learn and improve.


Scheduling Information:

I am currently not working and have no kids (yet). This makes it a very ideal time for me to study if we ignore the fact that I am currently 11 weeks pregnant. I am due in June, leaving me plenty of time to study from January to March.

Outside of studying, I try to keep fit with pole dancing lessons and would really like to return to those once we're out of lockdown. Ideally, I'd like to attend classes 3 times a week. However, it is likely that with the study load of the boot camp, I might have to put those on hold for 9 weeks.

I will do my best to do most of my studying in the week, leaving the weekends to relax, refresh & socialise with friends. However, when things pile up, I won't have any qualms putting social & other commitments on hold briefly so I can catch up.
